> **30-Second Overview**
> 
> Mega Financial Holding's predecessor was the Bank of Taiwan established in 1897, experiencing three eras: Japanese colonial rule, post-war state ownership, and privatization. Formed in 2006 through a merger of International Commercial Bank of China and Bank of Communications, it carries 127 years of banking heritage. With NT$4.6 trillion in assets and 68 locations across 38 countries, it's the best financial partner for Taiwan businesses' global expansion and the financial stock with the most shareholders—520,000 investors.

## Financial Memory on Chongqing South Road

Walking into Mega International Commercial Bank's headquarters on Chongqing South Road Section 1 in Taipei, few people realize this building harbors Taiwan's deepest banking history. The building's predecessor was the headquarters of Bank of Taiwan during Japanese rule—the birthplace of Taiwan's modern financial industry.

127 years ago, when the Japanese Governor-General's Office established Bank of Taiwan, they never imagined this "official bank" would become, over a century later, the financial backbone for Taiwanese businesses conquering global markets. They couldn't foresee that through three name changes and two major mergers, this banking bloodline would continue to today, becoming Taiwan's most internationalized financial group.

## Three Names, One Bloodline

Understanding Mega Financial requires grasping its complex "lineage"—a story of three bank names and two historic mergers.

In 1897, Bank of Taiwan was established during Japanese rule to issue currency and support the colonial economy as the official bank. After WWII, it became the national bank of the Republic of China, serving as the government's key tool for promoting economic development.

In 1971, the government established the separate "International Commercial Bank of China" (ICBC) to handle international financial services. At that time, Taiwan was experiencing economic takeoff and needed a professional foreign exchange bank to support export-oriented policies.

The third player was Bank of Communications, established by private interests in Beijing in 1907, moved to Shanghai in 1919, and relocated to Taiwan with the government in 1949, where it reopened.

Each bank had its specialty: Bank of Taiwan as the national bank, ICBC specializing in foreign exchange, and Bank of Communications serving industrial and commercial enterprises. But destiny's gears turned in 2006 when the government decided to merge ICBC and Bank of Communications, naming it "Mega International Commercial Bank" under the Mega Financial Holding Company system.

## Trust from 520,000 Shareholders

In April 2024, Mega Financial announced a cash dividend of NT$1.05, yielding 4.24%. The news delighted 520,000 shareholders—this is Taiwan's financial stock with the most shareholders and the most "people-friendly" financial investment option.

Why does Mega Financial enjoy such widespread investor trust? The answer lies in its steady management style.

As the leading public bank, Mega Financial inherited the cautious and conservative genes from its state-owned era. It doesn't pursue aggressive growth or high-risk investments but focuses on solid management of basic banking services. During the 2008 financial crisis, while European and American investment banks collapsed one after another, Mega Financial maintained stable profitability, becoming the stabilizing anchor of Taiwan's financial system.

More importantly is dividend stability. Mega Financial is among the few financial stocks that have consistently paid dividends for multiple years, particularly attractive to retirees seeking stable returns. This explains why it has 520,000 shareholders—from market vendors to tech professionals, everyone treats Mega Financial as a long-term investment safe haven.

## 68-Location Global Network Serving Taiwan Businesses

But Mega Financial's true competitive advantage lies in its unparalleled international network. With 68 business locations across 38 countries, this figure is unique in Taiwan's financial industry.

From Wall Street in New York to Ginza in Tokyo, from Sydney Harbor to London's financial district, Mega's signs are found throughout global financial centers. These locations aren't decorative—they're crucial support for Taiwanese businesses' international expansion.

Imagine: A Taiwanese electronics manufacturer wants to build a factory in Vietnam and needs local financing and foreign exchange hedging; a textile company wants to establish production lines in Mexico and needs cross-border capital allocation; a traditional manufacturer wants to transform into a multinational corporation and needs global cash management services. In these critical moments, Mega's international network becomes the most reliable financial partner for Taiwanese enterprises.

More importantly is the Taiwan business service experience. Through decades of cultivation, Mega has built a complete Taiwan business financial service system, from trade financing to investment management, from foreign exchange hedging to capital allocation, forming service capabilities that other banks find difficult to replicate.

## Outstanding 2024 Performance

Mega Financial delivered impressive results for 2024: after-tax net profit of NT$34.766 billion, a 5% increase and new historical high. More impressive is that this performance was achieved amid changing global interest rate environments and geopolitical tensions.

The NT$4.6 trillion asset scale makes Mega Financial Taiwan's second-largest bank, trailing only Bank of Taiwan. But compared to other private banks, Mega Financial's profitability and asset quality are quite impressive, with a capital adequacy ratio exceeding 13%, far above regulatory requirements.

Particularly noteworthy is overseas profit contribution. As Taiwanese businesses become increasingly internationalized, Mega Financial's overseas operations have grown rapidly, becoming an important source of group profits. This trend is expected to continue as Taiwan enterprises' internationalization steps won't cease.

## Market-Oriented Transformation of a Policy Bank

Mega Financial's success also reflects the achievements of Taiwan's state-owned enterprise market-oriented reforms. It maintains the advantages of public bank steady management while possessing the flexibility of market competition.

In policy implementation, Mega Financial actively supports the government's New Southbound Policy by strengthening deployments in Southeast Asia; in financial market stability, it plays an important stabilizer role; in SME financing, it fulfills its social responsibilities.

But it's not purely a policy tool. Facing challenges from pure internet banks and financial technology, Mega Financial actively promotes digital transformation, launching My Bank digital accounts, API open banking, and other innovative services. This balance between stability and innovation is precisely Mega Financial's competitive advantage.

## Future of Coexisting Challenges and Opportunities

Of course, Mega Financial faces considerable challenges. Long-term low interest rate environments compress banking industry net interest margins, financial technology disrupts traditional banking service models, international anti-money laundering regulations become increasingly strict—all testing Mega Financial's adaptability.

But opportunities are equally obvious. Continued internationalization of Taiwan businesses brings growth momentum for Mega Financial's cross-border financial services; ESG sustainable finance becomes a new trend with broad prospects for Mega Financial's green finance business; deepening Asian economic integration will make Mega Financial's Asia-Pacific regional deployment advantages more prominent.

Most importantly, as Taiwan's position in global supply chains rises, Taiwanese enterprises' international financial needs will only become more diversified and specialized. This is precisely Mega Financial's opportunity.

## 127 Years of Banking Heritage

From Bank of Taiwan in 1897 to Mega Financial Holding in 2024, 127 years of financial history witnessed Taiwan's complete transformation from agricultural society to technology island. It participated in economic construction during Japanese rule, supported post-war economic recovery, promoted Taiwan businesses' global expansion, and will accompany Taiwan's economy into the next century.

In the rapidly changing financial technology era, Mega Financial's value lies not in its scale but in the historical heritage it carries, its international network, and deep understanding of Taiwan's economy. These are core competitive advantages that other competitors find difficult to replicate.

The trust of 520,000 shareholders isn't accidental, a 68-location network across 38 countries wasn't built overnight, and 127 years of financial heritage can't be easily replaced. As Taiwan's financial industry faces new challenges today, Mega Financial remains the most trustworthy financial partner and an important force behind Taiwan enterprises' journey to the world.
